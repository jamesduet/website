Frequently Asked Questions

1. What is VRH?
VRH stands for Vehicle Revenue Hour. It’s the travel time between the pickup location and the drop-off location with a rider, when you are generating revenue.
2. How is VRH calculated?
At Duet, when a booking is created in our system, its VRH is the travel time estimated by Google Maps API using its pickup and drop-off addresses.

VRH is not the actual travel time that your driver claims. VRH is also not the same as your operating hours. VRH is typically a fraction of the operating hours, and smaller than the actual travel time as Google Maps APIs do not consider the real-time traffic time.
3. How much will your software cost me per month?
Providers on our platform pay about $45 to $55 per vehicle per month. Some of them operate 24/7.

you can email us (ride@duetinc.com) if you'd like an estimate for your particular service.
4. Will the canceled or no show trips be included in the cost calculation?
No show trips are included, but canceled trips are not.
5. Will my cost be different if I provide wheelchair trips vs stretcher trips?
No. The mobility mode doesn’t make any difference.
6. Will my cost be different if I provide long-distance, cross-state trips?
No. Your lodging and resting time are not included in our cost calculation.
7. Why do you use VRH for pricing?
We only want to earn money when you do.
8. What’s the benefit to me of using VRH?
You don’t have to pay for vehicles that are out of service. You pay less during slow periods (e.g., holidays). And you don’t have to keep your software vendor up to date on your fleet size.
