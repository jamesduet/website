Reporting And Billing Are Vital To Your Cash Flow Management

The most underestimated piece of NEMT service management is billing. Many new providers are not prepared for how long it takes to get paid. In this article, I will discuss NEMT payment and billing to help you understand how to manage the cashflow of your NEMT business.

By Jing Zhu, Cofounder & CEO at Duet
December 10, 2024

As a new provider in the NEMT services, one of the most common questions you may ask yourself is, "Where should I source my trips?" The answer is simple - everywhere and all. However, the more advanced answer depends on a combination of different factors.

Regardless of your size and experience, you need trips from all sources. Why? Because transport services are all about cashflow management. As a professional provider, you will have a bunch of bills to pay regardless of whether you provide any services or not. Your goal is two-fold - cover your cost and make a profit. You need revenue for both, and you cannot reach profit without covering the cost. Unless those trips cost you more to provide than not provide, then you can skip. Otherwise, you need all trips you can get to fill in your vehicle/driver hours.

The “best” source depends on both your local market and what kind of business you want to build. I’ll kick off this series by diving into private pay.

To optimize your profit margin, you need to balance and re-balance trips from different sources. Assuming you have no problem getting trips from private pay, facilities, hospitals, brokers, etc., now your emphasis needs to shift to maximize high margin trips to optimize your profitability.
